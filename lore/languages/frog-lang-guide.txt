
This is a guide of the Frog Language (spoken in the Lily Islands, W.I.P.)

Word Order: As in English, but with adjectives after nouns and adverbs after verbs. Word order cannot vary from Subject-Verb-Object.

Conjugation:

Uses suffixes:

-a or -na(if a word ends in a vowel) present/infinitive
-i or -ni(if a word ends in a vowel) past
-u or -nu(if a word ends in a vowel) future

Plural nouns end with -i or -ni (if a word ends in a vowel)

Orthography and Pronouncuation:

Letter - International Phonetic Alphabet - Englsih equivalent

Stress is always on the first syllable of a word.

m - m - as in the m in man
n - n - as in the n in nature
ŋ - ng - as in the ng in sing
p - p - as in the p in spin
t - t - as in the t in stack
k - k - as in the k in ski
' - ʔ - as in the - in uh-oh in received pronouncuation
f - f - as in the f in fax
s - s - as in the sum
h - h - as in the h in ham
r - ɹ - as in the r in crow in received pronouncuation, but slightly further foward in the mouth and without rounding the lips
j - j - as in the y in yam
w - w - as in the w in wind
l - l - as in the l in land
i - i - as in the i in free but shorter
u - u - as in the u in boot in received pronouncuation
a - a - as in the a in hat in received pronouncuation

There are no capital letters.
